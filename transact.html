Significance of using an Escrow

Escrow is a financial instrument held by a third party on behalf of two other parties who are completing a transaction. It's like a trust account held by a third party while all the seller and buyer's obligations are fulfilled.

Escrow is necessary for any domain name purchase because it protects the parties involved in the transaction. The time it takes to go from the beginning to the end of the escrow process varies. We use DAN.com's domain escrow service to ensure smooth transactions for domain buyers.

How the Escrow process works for domain names

Buyer and Seller agree regarding the transaction parameters (domain name and price), both parties agree that example.com will be sold by Seller and bought by Buyer for $$$, once agreed, Seller imports the buyer details and transaction details on DAN.com.

After successful import, Buyer receives a confirmation email from DAN.com, this email contains a link. On clicking the link Buyer is taken to DAN.com checkout page to process the transaction further. Buyer checks the transaction parameters and proceeds with the payment, on successful payment completion the funds are transfered to DAN.com escrow account.

After payment completion, DAN.com notifies the Seller that funds have been received, Seller then transfers the domain name to DAN.com holding account, subsequently the domain is transfered to the buyer. Dan.com guides the Buyer with the transfer process.

If the Seller fails to transfer domain name to DAN.com holding account, the buyer's funds which are still in Dan.com's escrow account are refunded.
